Functional Magnetic Resonance Induction

Understanding fMRI

Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) measures small blood flow changes associated with brain activity. It helps examine brain function, evaluate stroke effects, or guide treatments. fMRI can detect abnormalities missed by other techniques.

Preparation for FMRI

Inform your doctor about health issues, surgeries, allergies, or possible pregnancy. Most implants are safe, but disclose any devices or metal in your body. Follow specific guidelines on eating, drinking, and caffeine intake before the exam.

Common Uses of fMRI

Doctors use fMRI to understand brain function, assess surgical risks, and evaluate stroke, trauma, or degenerative disease effects. The FDA approves fMRI mainly for surgical planning, helping to map brain functions and monitor tumors.

  • Assess brain function and surgical risks
  • Evaluate stroke effects and monitor tumors
