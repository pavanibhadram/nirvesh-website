Introduction

Understanding Traumatic Brain Injury

Traumatic brain injury (TBI) occurs from a blow, bump, or jolt to the head or when an object enters the skull. Symptoms range from mild concussions to severe physical and psychological effects.

Long-Term Effects of TBI

Long-term effects of TBI depend on the injury's location and severity. Damage to specific brain regions can impair behavior, speech, visual processing, and memory, with severe cases causing paralysis, seizures, and mood swings.

Rehabilitation And
Treatment

Rehabilitation involves a multidisciplinary team providing therapy to improve daily functioning. Cognitive Rehabilitation Therapy (CRT) offers individualized programs to restore brain function and manage deficits, requiring further development and evaluation for effectiveness.

  • Multidisciplinary therapy improves daily functioning
  • CRT restores brain function
